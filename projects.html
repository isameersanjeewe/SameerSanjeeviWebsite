Thermlegy

Thermelgy- is an IoT based platform aimed to monitor energy and equipments for HVAC applications. The platform gets data from edge devices and provides the user with customised BI thereby helping them improve the operational efficency through direct control of the equipments all remotely. The direct and indrect savings achieved by the user through monitoring and managing their process is what drives thermelgy to enable energy as aservices.
As a founding member and a Product Manager, I wore multiple hats from gathering various stakeholder requirements to building Product road maps and managing the entire project. As our project scaled I switched from developing hardware IoT devices to developing an IoT platform / Web Application.


A small video about developing Thermelgy as a company and a business, this video was shot by me during a startup Incubator program in IIT-Madras.

Cloud-Pulze

CloudPulze- is a cloud cost optimization tool, designed to reduce cloud computing cost for business. The current version is compatible with GCP and can measure performance, start and stop cloud services instances like compute instance and Kubernetes.
As a Product Owner I coordinated the development of this product by understanding the customer needs and market needs. The MVP 1 was developed to be compatible with Google Cloud Platform specifically for Compute Engine and Kubernetes, the MVP 2 was to convert the web application to browser extension. I managed the project as a scrum master and helped develop the front end of the application.
The project is currently on hold due to limited engagement of stakeholders and investors.

StackByte

- "to bridge the gap between academia and industries for college students"


Why?
This idea came from a personal problem that I faced during my undergraduate education, due to the curriculum and the very short semester breaks at colleges didnâ€™t provide or encourage students to work on real-time projects at industries that could help in gaining hands-on experience and necessary skills as required for landing a first job.

How?
To create a platform that will drive on-hand learning experience, mentor them with relevant industry- standard skills to prepare them for the real job market. We decided on conducting a 6-week hack-a-thon where all registered students would be grouped based on their interests and assigned to a mentor who would coach them to work on a real-life project. During the final week we would also introduce the cohort to job searching process, building resume and networking. At the end of it the winner of the hackathon would get an opportunity to intern at one of the companies we partnered with.
